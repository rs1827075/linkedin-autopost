## Post text (edit freely, this is what gets published)

I have been looking into how Headless 360 is changing the way we bridge Slack and Salesforce through Hosted MCP Servers. For those of us working in Financial Services Cloud or complex environments, the promise here is creating a unified, secure conversational interface that lives right inside your Slack workflow. Instead of switching between tools, teams can now potentially execute Salesforce flows, query Tableau data, or pull specific CRM details directly through their chat interface. This matters because it reduces context switching and simplifies how non-technical users interact with complex data architecture. Imagine a scenario where a financial advisor needs an immediate client snapshot or an automated process trigger; having that accessible via a secure bot rather than navigating multiple screens could be a significant efficiency boost for front-office operations. It is an interesting shift toward more fluid data interaction, though it requires careful consideration of how these hosted servers handle authentication and internal security protocols. I am curious to see how this evolves for more granular integrations. Have you experimented with integrating MCP servers into your own Slack setups, or are you holding off to see how the security landscape matures?
